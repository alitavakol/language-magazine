Sands Film

The film Hunger is about the life and death of republican hunger striker Bobby Sands. The film was controversial because some considered Sands to be a terrorist as he was a member of the IRA. However, others saw him as a hero who took part in a peaceful protest. The film was directed by Steve McQueen, a British director.

Bobby Sands

Robert Gerard Sands, also known as Bobby Sands, was born on 9th March 1954. He was a Provisional Irish Republican Army volunteer and a member of the UK Parliament. He died on 5th May 1981 after a hunger strike in prison.

Speaking about the film, McQueen said that what Bobby Sands did wasn't right or wrong. "The film is about people’s decisions and the consequences of those decisions,” he added.

Actor Michael Fassbender (who was Stelios in the film 300), played the role of Sands. Fassbender was born in Germany but was brought up in Ireland.

Bobby Sands was 27 years old when he died. During the hunger strikes in the Maze Prison in 1981 he was elected as a member of the British Parliament. There were world-wide protests supporting him and other prisoners refused to eat too. Eventually, 10 protesters died while trying to get recognised as prisoners of war.
