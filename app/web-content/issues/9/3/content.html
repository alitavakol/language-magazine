Expressing Fear

This month, we’re looking at ways to express fear.

Expressing Fear

  • I’m scared of heights.
  • I’m terrified of being stuck in a lift.
  • I’m frightened of flying.
  • I’ve got a fear of flying.
  • I’m petrified of spiders.
  • I’m terrified of the dark.
  • I’m scared of him.
  • I’ve got this irrational fear of clowns.
  • I can’t bear to be in a room with spiders.
  • I can’t stand being in high places.
  • He's got a phobia about flying.
  • It was really scary!
  • It was frightening!

Trying to reassure someone

  • Come on, there’s nothing to worry about.
  • Don’t worry!
  • What’s the worst that could happen?
  • They can only say no.
  • Calm down!
  • Take it easy!

Desperate measures

  • Help!
  • Run!
  • Run for your life!

Dialogue: The Flight

Trevour is on a plane that’s about to take off. He’s feeling a bit nervous so he starts talking with Jim, the passenger next to him. [Listen and complete with the correct prepositions.]

T=Trevour   J=Jim

T: All right?
J: Morning.
T: Off to Thailand, are you?
J: Certainly am. And you?
T: Yep… erm… [laughs nervously] I just hope the plane doesn’t crash ………… we get there!
J: [laughing] That wouldn’t be a great start ………… the holiday, would it?
T: No. I hope it’s a smooth flight. I can’t stand the turbulence.
J: Oh, I love it! It’s like being ………… a rollercoaster!
T: It’s just that I get really scared during the take-off and landing.
J: Sounds like you’ve got a fear ………… flying.
T: I’m terrified of it!
J: Take it easy! You’ve got nothing to worry about! You’ve got more chance of dying ………… a car than a plane.
T: I know. I wish I could be as calm as you.
J: Well, if it makes you feel any better, I’ve got a few fears ………… my own.
T: Really?
J: Yeah, well, I’m scared of spiders, elevators, open spaces, fish, crowds, plus a few other things. Oh, and I also suffer ………… coulrophobia.
T: What’s that?
J: An irrational fear of clowns.
T: Clowns? So, what happens when you see one?
J: I tense …………. And if the clown comes too close, I start screaming. But I love flying!
T: Lucky you. I think I’ll order a drink to calm my nerves.
J: I’d prefer you didn’t.
T: Why?
J: I also suffer ………… methyphobia, an irrational fear of alcohol. Vodka, gin, brandy, beer, wine… Just saying the names gives me the creeps.
T: Oh, OK. Then, I’ll just read my book.
J: Best if you didn’t. Bibliophobia. Fear of books. If you open a book, I’ll break ………… in a cold sweat.
T: Oh. Erm, you aren’t afraid of germs, are you?
J: Germs? Are you kidding? Not at all!
T: Great, then you won’t mind holding my hand during take-off.
